Chapter 1: Chemical Reactions and Equations

Understanding how substances change and transform around us.

Welcome to the first chapter of Class 10 Science! Here, we will dive into the fascinating world of chemical reactions, learn about their types, and understand how to represent them using chemical equations. This forms the fundamental basis of chemistry.

1.1 What is a Chemical Reaction?

A chemical reaction is a process that leads to the chemical transformation of one set of chemical substances to another. Chemical reactions are all around us, from the rusting of iron to the digestion of food in our bodies.

Indicators of a Chemical Reaction:

  • Change in state
  • Change in color
  • Evolution of a gas
  • Change in temperature
  • Formation of a precipitate

1.2 Writing Chemical Equations

A chemical equation is the symbolic representation of a chemical reaction in the form of symbols and formulae, wherein the reactant entities are given on the left-hand side and the product entities on the right-hand side with a plus sign between the entities in both the reactant and product sides and an arrow that indicates the direction of the reaction.

Example: Magnesium + Oxygen → Magnesium Oxide

In symbolic form: Mg(s) + O2(g) → MgO(s)

Quick Check: What is the Law of Conservation of Mass?

The Law of Conservation of Mass states that mass in an isolated system is neither created nor destroyed by chemical reactions or physical transformations. This means, the total mass of the products equals the total mass of the reactants.
